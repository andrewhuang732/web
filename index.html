PROJECTS AND PUBLICATIONS


Using Machine Learning to Predict Cinderella Teams
For my CIS 519 (Machine Learning) final project, my team worked on using various regression, classification, and ordinal regression algorithms to predict the performance of low-seeded NCAA teams in the annual March Madness tournament. Through rigorous feature analysis and extraction, we were able to build a Naive Bayes classifier that outperformed ESPN analyst brackets. Implemented using Python through the libraries scikit learn and mord. Click here for the final paper, here for the poster, and here for the source code.

Projecting Index Returns
For my club Wharton Analytics Fellows, we consulted for Signet Financial Management, a firm with over $650M AUM. Due to the NDA that our team signed, I cannot disclose any results or code, but we used machine learning and principal component analysis to predict S&P500 and Russell indices through various macroeconomic data. We then used our results to optimize a portfolio for our client. We directly worked with the CEO of the company in developing our project, and presented our findings on a weekly basis.

Cracking the Vigenere Cipher
For my STAT 433 (Stochastic Processes) individual final project, I used the Metropolis Hastings algorithm (a Monte Carlo Markov Chain method) to decrypt the Vigenere cipher of a known length and an unknown length. I also developed optimized non-MCMC solutions as a baseline for comparison. I then decrypted various extensions, including Vigenere without spaces and Kasiski's codelength analysis. I wrote the code in R, which can be found here on my Github. Click here for the final project writeup.

Modeling the Stock Market
I used Twitter's API to data mine Tweets regarding specific companies. I then utilized natural language processing libraries to determine each company’s public sentiment and used statistical analysis to correlate and predict their stock prices. Implemented using Python. Here is the publication. Click here for the source code.
